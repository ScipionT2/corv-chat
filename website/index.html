FAQ

Common questions.

The core of Nova is free, open source, and always will be. The local AI runs on your hardware at zero cost. We're building an optional Nova Pro tier with cloud features and premium voices for those who want more power — but the free version is a complete product, not a demo.
Absolutely not. Every part of Nova runs on your local machine — the language model, speech recognition, and all processing. No internet connection is needed after the initial model download. Your conversations never leave your computer.
Nova runs best on Apple Silicon Macs (M1 or later) with at least 8GB of RAM. It uses Metal acceleration for fast inference. Intel Macs are supported but performance will be significantly slower.
Nova runs entirely offline with zero cost and complete privacy — unlike ChatGPT (cloud-based, subscription). Compared to Siri, Nova offers deeper conversations, screen awareness, and is fully customizable. The trade-off: cloud models may have broader knowledge, but Nova keeps everything on your machine.
Yes. Nova uses Ollama under the hood, which supports dozens of open-source models — Llama, Mistral, Phi, Gemma, and more. Switch models in settings based on your preference for speed, capability, or memory usage.
Yes. The full source code is available on GitHub. You can inspect it, modify it, contribute to it, or fork it for your own projects. Transparency is a core principle of Nova.
